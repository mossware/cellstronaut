What is Space Biology?

Space biology is the scientific study of how life adapts and changes in the unique environment of space. On Earth, all life has evolved under the constant pull of gravity. In space, factors like microgravity, increased radiation, and confined habitats create new challenges and opportunities for living organisms. By studying everything from tiny microbes to complex human systems, scientists can uncover fundamental insights into how life works.

A Brief Timeline of Milestones

1947

First Life in Space

Fruit flies are launched aboard a U.S. V-2 rocket, becoming the first animals to reach space and be recovered alive.

1957

First Animal to Orbit Earth

The Soviet dog Laika becomes the first animal to orbit the Earth, providing crucial data on a living creature's response to spaceflight.

1960

First Plants Grown in Space

Wheat, peas, and corn are successfully grown from seeds aboard Sputnik 4, proving that plants can germinate in microgravity.

1983

Spacelab Establishes Research Platform

The first Spacelab mission provides a sophisticated laboratory for detailed human and cellular biology experiments in orbit.

2015

First Food Grown & Eaten in Space

Astronauts aboard the ISS harvest and eat 'Outredgeous' red romaine lettuce grown in the Veggie habitat.

2019

NASA Twins Study Concludes

NASA completes its landmark study of twin astronauts, providing unprecedented insights into how a year in space affects the human body.
